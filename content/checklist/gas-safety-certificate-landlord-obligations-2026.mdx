Gas safety is one of the most important responsibilities you have as a landlord. A faulty gas appliance can cause carbon monoxide poisoning, fires, and explosions, putting your tenants' lives at risk. The law is clear: every landlord who rents out a property with gas appliances must hold a valid gas safety certificate. Failing to comply can result in heavy fines, criminal prosecution, and an inability to regain possession of your property.

This guide explains exactly what you need to do to meet your gas safety obligations in 2026.

## The Legal Requirement

Your obligation to maintain gas safety in rental properties comes from the **Gas Safety (Installation and Use) Regulations 1998**. These regulations apply to all landlords in England, Scotland, and Wales who let properties with gas appliances, fittings, or flues.

Under these regulations, you must:

- **Arrange an annual gas safety check** on every gas appliance, flue, and pipework in your rental property
- **Use a Gas Safe registered engineer** to carry out all checks and repairs
- **Provide a copy of the gas safety record** to existing tenants within 28 days of the check, or to new tenants before they move in
- **Keep records** of each safety check for at least two years

These requirements apply regardless of whether you own one property or fifty. There are no exemptions based on the number of properties you let.

## What the Gas Safety Certificate Covers

A gas safety check is a thorough inspection of all gas appliances and installations in your property. The engineer will examine:

### Gas Appliances

- **Boilers** (combination, system, and regular boilers)
- **Gas fires** and fireplaces
- **Gas cookers and hobs**
- **Gas water heaters**
- **Gas tumble dryers**

### Flues and Ventilation

- **Flue terminals** and their condition
- **Ventilation adequacy** for each appliance
- **Flue flow** to ensure gases are being properly expelled
- **Spillage tests** to check combustion products aren't entering the room

### Pipework

- **Visible gas pipework** for signs of corrosion or damage
- **Gas meter connections** and emergency controls
- **Pipe joints** and fittings for leaks

### Safety Devices

- **Flame failure devices** on cookers and fires
- **Carbon monoxide detectors** (recommended, and increasingly required)
- **Gas isolation valves** and emergency controls

## Who Can Issue a Gas Safety Certificate

Only a **Gas Safe registered engineer** can legally carry out a gas safety check and issue a certificate. Gas Safe Register replaced CORGI in 2009 as the official registration body for gas engineers in the UK.

### How to Verify an Engineer

Before allowing anyone to work on gas appliances in your property:

1. **Ask for their Gas Safe ID card** — every registered engineer carries one
2. **Check the card is current** — cards have an expiry date
3. **Verify the card number** online at GasSafeRegister.co.uk or call 0800 408 5500
4. **Check their qualifications** — the back of the card lists which appliances they are qualified to work on
5. **Ensure they are qualified for the specific work** — an engineer qualified for boilers may not be qualified for gas fires

### Finding a Gas Safe Engineer

You can find registered engineers through:

- The Gas Safe Register website (GasSafeRegister.co.uk)
- Local recommendations from other landlords
- Your letting agent or property management company
- Landlord association recommendations

It is worth building a relationship with a reliable engineer who can handle your annual checks and any emergency repairs.

## The Annual Renewal Requirement

Gas safety certificates must be renewed **every 12 months**. The renewal date is based on the date of the previous check, not the date of the tenancy.

### The "MOT-Style" Flexibility

Since April 2018, you can arrange your gas safety check up to **two months before the expiry date** without losing time on your certificate. If your current certificate expires on 1 September, you can have the check done any time from 1 July onwards, and the new certificate will still run until 1 September the following year.

This flexibility means you do not need to leave it until the last minute. Booking early gives you a buffer in case the engineer finds faults that need follow-up work.

### What Happens If You Miss the Deadline

If your gas safety certificate lapses, you are immediately in breach of the law. There is no grace period. From the moment the certificate expires, you are committing an offence for every day the property remains occupied without a valid certificate.

## What Happens During an Inspection

A typical gas safety check takes between 30 and 60 minutes, depending on the number of appliances. Here is what the engineer will do:

### The Inspection Process

1. **Visual inspection** of all gas appliances, pipework, and flues
2. **Operating pressure checks** on each appliance
3. **Gas tightness test** on the pipework (checking for leaks)
4. **Flue flow test** to ensure combustion products are being safely removed
5. **Ventilation check** to confirm adequate air supply
6. **Safety device checks** on each appliance
7. **Combustion analysis** to check appliances are burning gas efficiently

### After the Inspection

The engineer will complete a **Landlord Gas Safety Record** (also called a CP12). This document includes:

- The address of the property
- The name and registration number of the engineer
- The date of the inspection
- Details of each appliance checked
- The results of each check
- Any defects found and remedial action taken
- A statement of whether each appliance is safe to use

If any appliance is found to be unsafe, the engineer will classify it as either **At Risk** (AR) or **Immediately Dangerous** (ID). An immediately dangerous appliance will be disconnected on the spot and must not be used until repaired.

## Tenant Rights

Your tenants have specific rights regarding gas safety:

### Right to a Copy of the Certificate

- **Existing tenants** must receive a copy within 28 days of the annual check
- **New tenants** must receive a copy before they move in
- Tenants can request a copy at any time during the tenancy

### Right to Safe Appliances

Tenants have the right to live in a property where all gas appliances are safe and properly maintained. If a tenant reports a gas smell or suspects a fault, you must arrange for a Gas Safe engineer to attend promptly.

### Right to Refuse Unsafe Appliances

A tenant is within their rights to refuse to use a gas appliance that has been deemed unsafe. If an engineer has condemned an appliance, neither you nor the tenant should attempt to use it.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

The penalties for failing to comply with gas safety regulations are severe and can be life-changing.

### Fines

- Failure to conduct an annual gas safety check: **unlimited fine** in the Magistrates' Court
- Historically, fines of up to **£6,000** have been common, though courts can impose higher penalties
- Each offence (per property, per breach) can be penalised separately

### Criminal Prosecution

- Non-compliance is a **criminal offence**
- You can be prosecuted under the Health and Safety at Work Act 1974
- In cases where a tenant is injured or killed due to a faulty gas appliance, you could face **manslaughter charges**

### Impact on Possession Proceedings

- Without a valid gas safety certificate, you may be unable to serve a valid notice to seek possession
- Under the Renters' Rights Act 2025, compliance with gas safety obligations is a prerequisite for certain grounds for possession
- Courts may refuse to grant a possession order if you cannot demonstrate compliance

### Insurance Implications

- Your landlord insurance may be **voided** if you do not hold a valid gas safety certificate
- Any claims related to gas incidents could be rejected
- Building and contents cover may be affected

## Serving the Certificate to Tenants

You must provide the gas safety record to tenants in a way that can be evidenced:

### Best Practice for Serving

- **Email** the certificate as a PDF attachment (keep a record of the sent email)
- **Hand-deliver** a paper copy and have the tenant sign a receipt
- **Post** a copy via recorded delivery if the tenant is not available in person
- **Upload** it to a property management platform where the tenant can access it

Whatever method you use, keep proof that the certificate was served. If a dispute arises, you need to demonstrate that the tenant received the document.

## Record Keeping Requirements

You must keep copies of gas safety records for a minimum of **two years**. However, best practice is to keep them for the duration of the tenancy and at least six years afterwards, as this is the limitation period for most civil claims.

### What to Keep

- The original gas safety record (CP12) from each annual check
- Proof of service to tenants (email confirmations, signed receipts)
- Records of any remedial work carried out
- Invoices and receipts from Gas Safe engineers
- Correspondence with tenants about gas safety matters

### Storage

Store records securely, either as physical copies in a fireproof location or as digital copies backed up to the cloud. Digital storage is generally more practical, especially if you manage multiple properties.

## What to Do If a Tenant Refuses Access

Occasionally, a tenant may refuse or repeatedly fail to provide access for the annual gas safety check. This does not excuse you from your legal obligation.

### Steps to Take

1. **Write to the tenant** explaining the legal requirement and the importance of the check for their safety
2. **Offer multiple dates and times** to make it as convenient as possible
3. **Give at least 24 hours' written notice** of your intention to enter (or more if your tenancy agreement specifies)
4. **Keep records** of every attempt to arrange access, including letters, emails, and phone calls
5. **Send a formal letter** by recorded delivery warning that failure to allow access could put their safety at risk
6. **Seek legal advice** if the tenant continues to refuse after reasonable efforts

### Building a Defence

If you have made every reasonable effort to gain access and can evidence this, you may be able to use this as a defence in the event of enforcement action. However, you should continue trying to arrange the check and never simply give up.

## New Build Exemptions

There is a common misconception that new build properties are exempt from gas safety checks. This is **not the case**. If a new build property has gas appliances installed, it requires a gas safety check before it is let, just like any other property.

What may differ is the timing of the first check. If the property is brand new and has never been occupied, the developer's installation certificates may cover the initial period. However, once the first tenant moves in, the 12-month cycle begins from the date of the developer's certificate or the first gas safety check, whichever is earlier.

## LPG and Other Gas Types

Gas safety obligations are not limited to mains natural gas. They also cover:

### LPG (Liquefied Petroleum Gas)

- Properties using bottled gas or LPG tanks are fully covered by the regulations
- LPG appliances require a Gas Safe engineer with specific LPG qualifications
- The same annual check requirements apply

### Calor Gas and Portable Heaters

- If you supply portable gas heaters as part of the let, these must be included in the annual check
- Best practice is to avoid supplying portable gas heaters due to the additional safety risks

### Gas Meters

- Pre-payment gas meters and standard meters are included in the scope of the check
- The engineer will check the meter installation as part of the pipework inspection

## Practical Tips for Staying Compliant

### Set Up Reminders

The easiest way to miss a gas safety deadline is to forget about it. Set up calendar reminders at least two months before the certificate expires so you have time to book and complete the check.

### Build a Relationship with an Engineer

Having a trusted Gas Safe engineer who knows your properties makes the process smoother. They will understand the appliance configurations, know the access arrangements, and can flag potential issues early.

### Include Gas Safety in Your Tenancy Agreement

Your tenancy agreement should include clauses about the tenant's obligation to allow reasonable access for gas safety checks and to report any gas safety concerns promptly.

### Check Your Appliances Between Inspections

While the formal check is annual, keep an eye on your gas appliances during routine property visits. Look for signs of discolouration, soot marks, or unusual smells that could indicate a problem.

### Consider Carbon Monoxide Detectors

While not always a legal requirement in every room, installing carbon monoxide detectors near gas appliances is strongly recommended and increasingly expected by courts and insurers. The Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Alarm (Amendment) Regulations 2022 require a carbon monoxide alarm in any room with a fixed combustion appliance (excluding gas cookers).

## Conclusion

Gas safety is not an area where you can afford to cut corners. The legal requirements are clear, the penalties are severe, and the potential consequences of a gas incident are devastating. By arranging your annual check on time, using a properly qualified Gas Safe engineer, serving the certificate to your tenants, and keeping thorough records, you protect both your tenants and yourself.
